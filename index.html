District Dance Battle Telemetry

District Dance Battle is a rhythm game we developed in 2019. After some time, we decided to do some Telemetry tests to ensure some suspicions we had. These are the results.

RESULTS OF THE STUDY

Distances total

This image shows the total distances in the inputs the players made. By distance we mean the accuracy of the players when hitting correctly a note in the game. We divided it with 2 points of separation for more accuracy in the study.

Max Combo mean

After the study, we can see that the players tend to obtain more combo in the early songs. The result was expected because the early levels are easier that the ones later in the game.

Combo time mean

On the other hand, we observed that the levels where the players maintain the combo the most amount of time are some of the extra songs. We can explain this by pointing out that the design in the extra levels are more consistent that the first ones we made.

Times completed each level

This graphic didn't suprise us. We knew from previous experience which were the most popular levels, and we can see it yet again in this graphic.

Times lost each level

Similar to the previous graphic, we also knew which levels were the most difficult and the ones where the players failed the most. We can see it again in this graphic.

Scores mean

Same as before. We can see in this graphic that the players get higher scores in the early levels, and less score in the difficult levels. Some levels have infimum scores due to the players losing right away when starting these levels.

Input type

Surprisingly, players prefer playing with controllers but with the Arrows option. This option lets players play with XBox or PS4 controllers but on but on both sides of the screen it shows arrows.

Mistakes in level 2

This graphic shows the mistakes made by all players in level 2 - Eminemcia. We show this one as an example because it is the most significative, but we have one of each level. Due to the lack of testers, results are not significative enough, but with more tests we will be able to obtain better results.

Dialogues skipped

Players don't usually like reading dialogues. But this graphic shows that players don't skip dialogues. Why? Because they don't know which button is the skip button, or at least that's what wh think it is happening.
